## LlumDocs feature guide

This guide explains what each LlumDocs capability does and how you can use it in everyday work.
Use it together with the main `README.md` and `docs/GUI_SCREENSHOTS.md` to decide which feature to use in each situation.

---

## Text translation

- **What it does**: translates text between Catalan, Spanish, and English while keeping the meaning and tone as close to the original as possible.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Paste or type the text you want to translate.
  - Choose the source and target languages (or let LlumDocs detect the source automatically if available in your setup).
  - Run the translation and copy the result into your email, document, or system.
- **When it helps**:
  - Answering customer emails in their preferred language.
  - Reusing internal content (policies, FAQs, templates) across different regions.
  - Quickly checking the meaning of content you receive in another language.

---

## Plain‑language rewrites

- **What it does**: turns complex or formal text into clear, simple explanations for non‑experts.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Paste the original text.
  - Choose how simple you want the language to be.
  - Review the rewritten version and adjust details if needed.
- **When it helps**:
  - Explaining technical or legal concepts to customers or colleagues.
  - Writing help centre articles and internal documentation.
  - Preparing brief explanations for managers and stakeholders.

---

## Technical‑tone rewrites

- **What it does**: rewrites content so it sounds more formal and technical, suitable for professional or specialist audiences.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Paste your draft (for example, meeting notes or a rough description).
  - Indicate the type of audience or domain if your setup exposes this option.
  - Use the rewritten text in reports, specifications, or proposals.
- **When it helps**:
  - Turning working notes into polished documents.
  - Preparing technical sections for tenders or project documentation.
  - Aligning text with the language your professional audience expects.

---

## Company‑tone emails

- **What it does**: converts short prompts or rough drafts into complete, professional emails that reflect your organisation’s tone.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Paste a short description of what you want to say or the rough email you already wrote.
  - Choose the desired tone (for example, more serious/urgent or calm/professional) and language.
  - Review the generated email and personalise any details before sending.
- **When it helps**:
  - Replying to customers quickly while keeping a consistent brand voice.
  - Drafting sensitive messages that need careful wording.
  - Standardising communication across teams.

---

## Document summaries

- **What it does**: condenses long documents into short, easy‑to‑scan summaries.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Upload a file or paste the text you want to summarise.
  - Choose whether you need a brief overview, a detailed breakdown, or an executive summary.
  - Use the result to decide what to read in depth or to share a quick brief with others.
- **When it helps**:
  - Reviewing contracts, reports, policies, or meeting notes.
  - Preparing talking points for calls and presentations.
  - Catching up on long documents you received while you were away.

---

## Keyword extraction

- **What it does**: identifies the main topics, terms, and phrases in a piece of text.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Paste or upload the content you want to analyse.
  - If available, choose how many keywords or phrases you want.
  - Use the list for tagging, search, or quick orientation.
- **When it helps**:
  - Tagging documents in internal systems or shared folders.
  - Building quick topic overviews for research or analysis.
  - Spotting recurring themes in long reports or email threads.

---

## Image description

- **What it does**: creates natural‑language descriptions of images, scanned pages, and screenshots.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Upload an image.
  - Choose whether you want a short caption or a more detailed description (when available).
  - Copy the description into your system, caption field, or documentation.
- **When it helps**:
  - Adding descriptive text for accessibility.
  - Documenting visual evidence such as photos or screenshots.
  - Quickly understanding images embedded in documents and presentations.

---

## Email and ticket intelligence

- **What it does**: helps you triage and understand emails by suggesting routing, highlighting phishing risk, and estimating sentiment.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Paste the email content (and subject if relevant) into the email intelligence view.
  - Review the suggested category (for example, support, billing, sales, HR, or IT incident).
  - Check whether the message is likely to be safe or suspicious and see its overall tone.
- **When it helps**:
  - Managing shared inboxes or ticket queues more efficiently.
  - Prioritising which messages need urgent attention.
  - Reducing the risk of acting on phishing or malicious emails.

---

## Document data extraction

- **What it does**: reads structured business documents such as delivery notes, bank statements, and payroll documents, and extracts the key information they contain.
- **Typical steps**:
  - Upload the document and select the type (for example, delivery note, bank statement, or payroll).
  - Let LlumDocs process the file and present the extracted fields.
  - Export or copy the results into your internal tools or spreadsheets.
- **When it helps**:
  - Speeding up financial and payroll checks.
  - Reducing manual data entry for back‑office teams.
  - Creating structured records from scanned or emailed documents.
